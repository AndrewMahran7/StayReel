Today my first iOS app was officially accepted into the App Store.



It brought me back to 2020.



During COVID, I built a stupid Unity game called Ball Drop. I pulled multiple all-nighters trying to figure out how to push it to the Google Play Store. When it finally went live, I was unbelievably proud.



By the end of its life, it had four users:

• Me

• A family member

• Two random downloads from Pakistan



It was buggy. I eventually stopped maintaining it.



But it was the first project I ever took from idea → code → publishing.



My dad has always told me:

“Stop starting things. Finish something.”



Ball Drop was the first time I did.



After that, I worked on bigger, more ambitious projects. My last one, Explicitly, pushed the boundaries of what I could technically build. I spent days building data pipelines, scraping and loading datasets, training models, validating outputs. It was complex. It was ambitious.



But it never reached the final vision.



I recently saw a video describing two types of founders:



The founder who pushes the limits of technology and then tries to sell it.



The founder who finds a proven need and builds a clean solution for it, even if it’s not technologically impressive.



For a long time, I was the first type.



StayReel is me trying the second.



I saw apps like Reports+ charging premium subscriptions for follower analytics with hundreds of thousands of reviews. There was already demand. The problem wasn’t technological complexity. It was execution.



So I built StayReel in three days with a clear vision and AI-assisted development.



It’s not groundbreaking technology.

It’s not a research paper.

It’s not pushing the limits of machine learning.



It’s finished.



That feels different.



I’m currently running a private beta before officially releasing it on the App Store. During beta, it’s completely free while I gather feedback and improve the experience.


From a buggy Unity game with four users to shipping on the App Store.



Still building.

But now, finishing.
